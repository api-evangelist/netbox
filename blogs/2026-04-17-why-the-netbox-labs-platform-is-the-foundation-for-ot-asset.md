<p>Operational technology (OT) environments power critical infrastructure. Electric utilities, manufacturing plants, energy and transportation networks, and water systems all depend on OT, and these environments behave nothing like IT. Equipment lifecycles span decades, networks are fragile and highly sensitive to interference, and downtime translates directly into safety incidents, lost production, and interrupted service.</p>
<h2>Challenges the NetBox Labs Platform Solves</h2>
<p><strong>Low trust in inventory and connectivity data.</strong> Spreadsheets, tribal knowledge, and stale records sit at the center of too many OT environments. Engineers can&#8217;t troubleshoot, plan upgrades, or automate with any confidence when the source data is suspect from the start.</p>
<p><strong>IT-centric tools that don&#8217;t go network-deep.</strong> Many utilities already run an ITSM or CMDB platform, but those systems were built around IT assets and business services. They rarely capture the granular device, port, circuit, and physical connectivity detail OT engineers actually work with day to day.</p>
<p><strong>Fragmented records across multi-vendor environments.</strong> WAN controllers, field-area network platforms, and OT monitoring systems each own a slice of the environment. No single one sees the whole network, and stitching their exports together by hand is slow, error-prone, and never complete.</p>
<p><strong>Drift and inconsistent changes.</strong> Undocumented changes pile up between audits. By the time drift surfaces, it&#8217;s in the middle of an incident, a compliance review, or a modernization project that was supposed to be routine. Teams end up debugging reality against records they already knew were out of date.</p>
<h2>How the NetBox Labs Platform Empowers Network and Infrastructure Teams</h2>
<p>NetBox is the open-source standard for network and infrastructure data. NetBox Labs is the commercial steward. Utilities get the transparency and community of open source with the support, security, and scale enterprise operations demand.</p>
<p>Modeling what you have and how it connects is only the starting value the NetBox Labs platform delivers. The platform feeds that context directly into <a href="https://netboxlabs.com/products/netbox-discovery/">NetBox Discovery</a>, <a href="https://netboxlabs.com/products/netbox-assurance/">NetBox Assurance</a>, and the automation and operations workflows that utilities already run, so the same data that supports documentation also drives real work. For utilities, that shift shows up in three places.</p>
<h3>1. Build a CMDB Engineers Actually Trust</h3>
<p>Traditional CMDBs rarely hold up in utility environments because they were not built to model network relationships, physical connectivity, or operational detail at the depth engineers need. The NetBox Labs platform is designed as a network and infrastructure CMDB first. Devices, sites, circuits, IP addressing, interfaces, and both physical and logical connections sit in a single, consistent data model.</p>
<p>For utility network and OT teams, that means engineers can trust the data they are working from when they troubleshoot, plan upgrades, or design automation. The NetBox Labs platform captures the context that matters, including how devices are connected, where they are deployed, what configurations they run, and how they fit into the broader grid network. A shared, authoritative system of record reduces reliance on spreadsheets and tribal knowledge, and it gives teams a foundation to scale IP-based transport, field-area networks, and substation connectivity as grid modernization accelerates.</p>
<h3>2. Keep the System of Record Accurate</h3>
<p>NetBox Assurance continuously compares the system of record against what is actually running on the network. It detects drift, flags inconsistencies, and drives reconciliation, so teams can review and resolve discrepancies during normal operations instead of during an outage or an audit.</p>
<p>For utilities operating under NERC CIP, this maps directly to the controls auditors care about. CIP-002 requires utilities to identify and categorize their BES Cyber Systems, with that inventory reviewed and approved at least every 15 months. For medium- and high-impact systems, CIP-010 requires a documented configuration baseline for each BES Cyber Asset, with every change authorized and tracked. The NetBox Labs platform is the system of record that inventory sits in, and continuous reconciliation is the control that proves the baseline hasn&#8217;t drifted.</p>
<p>NetBox Assurance is paired natively with <a href="https://netboxlabs.com/products/netbox-discovery/">NetBox Discovery</a>, which collects inventory data from systems like <a href="https://netboxlabs.com/blog/announcing-general-availability-of-the-vmware-vcenter-integration-for-netbox-enterprise-and-netbox-cloud/">VMware vCenter</a>, <a href="https://netboxlabs.com/blog/netbox-integration-cisco-catalyst-center-available-customer-preview/">Cisco Catalyst Center</a>, <a href="https://netboxlabs.com/blog/netbox-integration-juniper-mist-available-customer-preview/">Juniper Mist</a>, and many others. Discovery, assurance, and automation close the loop, and the network documented in the NetBox Labs platform stays aligned with operational reality.</p>
<h3>3. Power NOC Modernization and Automation at Scale</h3>
<p>NOC teams relying on dispatch-only workflows cannot keep up with modern grid operations. The NetBox Labs platform gives them accurate, contextual infrastructure data in one place, so device inventory, topology, configuration, and lifecycle data correlate against every alert. Operators can isolate likely root causes faster, understand blast radius before they act, and prioritize remediation before small problems become outages. This matters most in multi-vendor utility environments, where any single controller or monitoring tool only sees part of the picture.</p>
<p>As utilities adopt automation and zero-touch provisioning, the NetBox Labs platform becomes the layer that makes those programs reliable. Orchestration and automation platforms pull inventory and intended-state data from the NetBox Labs platform to drive consistent provisioning, change validation, and rollback across transport, substation, and field networks. The result is faster deployment, fewer misconfigurations, and greater confidence as IP-based infrastructure and device density expand across the grid.</p>

<p>At an investor-owned electric utility in the western United States, the NetBox Labs platform was adopted as part of a broader network operations center modernization program. The utility needed a reliable, network and infrastructure system of record to support proactive operations, automation, and large-scale upgrades across transport and field networks. Centralizing inventory, topology, and configuration data in the NetBox Labs platform let the organization move beyond dispatch-only workflows and lay the foundation for zero-touch provisioning and data-driven troubleshooting, capabilities the existing CMDB approach could not deliver.</p>
